Management tool to digitize medical records in Pakistan and assist physicians before, during, and after patient vists

My Role:

I worked on this project as one of two product designers. I turned initial concepts and user research into solutions and app features. Throughout the process, I made sketches, wireframes, and interactive prototypes for the Patient First team to present and demo to stakeholders.

Tools:

Figma, Photoshop

Project Timeline:

Jul 2020 - Aug 2020

Client:

Patient First

The Challenge

Approximately 87% of medical data is recorded manually in Pakistan. This results in loss of medical history, many unfilled prescriptions, and irregular monitoring of chronic diseases. The lack of organized and well-kept data makes it difficult for physicians to provide their patients with accurate diagnoses and necessary healthcare. Our client tasked us with designing and prototyping a mobile healthcare app for Pakistani physicians and patients in order to digitize medical records.

The Solution

The Patient First app is a digital medical record tool that primarily assists the physician before, during, and after a patient visit. The app allows the physician to quickly access a patient's medical history, fill out visit details, and help book their patient's next visit. While there is a separate interface that patients can access and interact with, for the purposes of this case study, I will mainly focus on the physician interface design process.

The Design Process

User Research
Physician Interviews

Our client provided us with interviews with Pakistani physicians that they gathered over the last year. Although we were unable to interview them ourselves, the provided transcripts gave us detailed insight into their user needs, pain points, and work flow. The following takeaways helped shape our designing mindset later throughout the process.

Key Takeaways
  1. Physicians need a no-frills, clean, and straightforward interface to accomplish their tasks quickly
  2. Physicians must be able to handle all aspects of the visit on their interfance because patients may not readily have smartphones to access the Patient First app
Competitor Analysis

The in-depth competitor analysis is confidential, however further explanation can be provided upon request. I researched and compared other Pakistani medical platforms to evaluate their features, strengths, weaknesses, and competitive positioning.

User Persona

(click image to enlarge)

Based on our initial research, we created user personas for our two user segements: physicians and patients. Although our main target user is the physician, we made a persona for the patient to make sure we considered all facets of the app. Our physician persona, Dr. Fatema Asim, is looking for an effective method to organize her patients' medical records and provide better diagnoses. Our patient persona, Faraz Ali, wants to make sure his family's medical history is well documented and fears being unable to recover lost information.

Taking it a step further, we developed the following physician user stories in order to narrow down our focus:

As a physician, I want to
  1. quickly access patient information so that I am better prepared to provide my patient with a diagnosis/prescription
  2. efficiently carry out patient visits from start to finish so that I can provide my patients with the care they need
  3. easily schedule appointments for my patients so that I can stay organized and help my patients keep track of their health

Creating these user personas helped us empathize with our users' pains and needs. During our ideation phase, I constantly referred back to our user personas and stories to make sure that we addressed these aspects.
