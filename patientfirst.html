Management tool for physicians and patients in Pakistan to digitize and keep track of medical records

My Role:

I worked on this project as one of two product designers. I turned initial concepts and user research into wireframes, and later interactive prototypes for the Patient First team to present and demo to stakeholders.

Tools:

Figma, Photoshop

Year:

2020

Client:

Patient First

The Challenge

Approximately 87% of medical data is recorded manually in Pakistan. This results in loss of medical history, many unfilled prescriptions, and irregular monitoring of chronic diseases. Our client tasked us with designing and prototyping a mobile and tablet healthcare app for physicians and patients to digitize medical records.

The Solution

The Patient First app is a digital medical record tool that has different interfaces for physicians and patients. While both physicians and patients can access medical history, the physician interface focuses on assisting the physician before, during, and after a patient visit, while the patient interface focuses on helping the patient take their health into their own hands.

The Design Process

User Research
Physician Interviews

Our client provided us with interviews with Pakistani physicians that they gathered over the last year. Although we were unable to interview them ourselves, the provided interviews gave us detailed insight on their user needs, pain points, and workflow. The following takeaways helped shape our designing mindset later throughout the process.

Key Takeaways
  1. Physicians need a no-frills, clean, and straightforward interface to accomplish their tasks quickly
  2. Patients may not readily have smartphones to access the Patient First app
Competitor Analysis

The in-depth competitor analysis is confidential. I researched and compared other Pakistani medical platforms to evaluate their features, strengths, weaknesses, and competitive positioning.

User Persona

(click image to enlarge)

Based on our initial research, we created user personas for our two user segements: physicians and patients. Our physician persona, Dr. Fatema Asim, is looking for an effective method to organize her patients' medical records and provide better diagnoses. Our patient persona, Faraz Ali, wants to make sure his family's medical history is well documented and fears being unable to recover lost information.

Creating these user personas helped us empathize with our users' pains and needs. During our ideation phase, I constantly referred back to our user personas to make sure that we addressed these aspects.

User Flow

Our client provided us with a list of feature requirements that they wanted us to include in our designs. Since the client had an upcoming deadline to meet with stakeholders, they wanted us to priortize designing the physician experience.

After discussing the requirements, we drafted a user flow for the physician interface to plan out and guide our design process. Throughout our design journey, we continually made edits to the user flow to reflect new user stories that were added along the way.

Low-Fidelity Sketches

I made these sketches to show our client preliminary layouts for the information needed on certain screens. The main goal was to confirm the general layout and get client feedback before developing mid-fidelity wireframes. Our client appreciated the sketches as they helped visually communicate what I envisioned for specific screens.
